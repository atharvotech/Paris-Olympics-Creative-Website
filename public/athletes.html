The Great Athletes of India

Unique Stories
Milkha Singh
Sport: Athletics (Track and Field)
Story: Known as the "Flying Sikh," Milkha Singh's journey from a war-torn childhood to becoming one of India's greatest athletes is truly inspiring. He lost his family during the Partition of India and faced numerous hardships. Despite these challenges, he went on to represent India in the 1956, 1960, and 1964 Olympics. His near-miss for a medal in the 400m final at the 1960 Rome Olympics remains one of the most memorable moments in Indian sports history.
Mary Kom
Sport: Boxing
Story: Mary Kom, a six-time world champion, grew up in a poor family in Manipur. She faced numerous obstacles, including financial difficulties and societal barriers, to pursue her passion for boxing. Her determination and hard work led her to win a bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics, making her the first Indian woman boxer to achieve this feat.
Abhinav Bindra
Sport: Shooting
Story: Abhinav Bindra made history by becoming the first Indian to win an individual gold medal at the Olympics. He achieved this milestone in the Men's 10m Air Rifle event at the 2008 Beijing Olympics. His journey is marked by his relentless pursuit of perfection and his dedication to the sport.
PV Sindhu
Sport: Badminton
Story: PV Sindhu's rise to fame is a story of hard work and perseverance. She became the first Indian woman to win an Olympic silver medal in badminton at the 2016 Rio Olympics. She followed this up with a bronze medal at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, making her the first Indian woman to win two Olympic medals.
Sushil Kumar
Sport: Wrestling
Story: Sushil Kumar's journey from a small village in Haryana to becoming a two-time Olympic medallist is truly inspiring. He won a bronze medal at the 2008 Beijing Olympics and a silver medal at the 2012 London Olympics. His achievements have inspired many young wrestlers in India.
Deepa Malik
Sport: Para Athletics
Story: Deepa Malik became the first Indian woman to win a medal in the Paralympic Games. She won a silver medal in the shot put event at the 2016 Rio Paralympics. Despite being paralyzed from the waist down, her determination and resilience have made her a role model for many.
Neeraj Chopra
Sport: Javelin Throw
Story: Neeraj Chopra's historic gold medal win at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics made him the first Indian track-and-field athlete to achieve this feat. His journey from a small village in Haryana to the global stage is a testament to his hard work and dedication.
