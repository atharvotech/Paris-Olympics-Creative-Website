Olympics History

The modern Olympic Games started in 1896.

The first modern Olympics were held in Athens, Greece.

The first Olympic champion was James Connolly from the USA, who won the triple jump event.

Highlights of the First Modern Olympics

  • Dates: April 6 to April 15, 1896
  • Number of Nations: 14
  • Number of head: 241
  • Number of Events: 43

Notable Winners

Some of the notable winners of the first modern Olympics include:

  • Spyridon Louis (Greece) - Marathon
  • Thomas Burke (USA) - 100 meters and 400 meters
  • Alfréd Hajós (Hungary) - Swimming (100 meters and 1200 meters freestyle)

Interesting Facts

Here are some interesting facts about the first modern Olympics:

  • The games were held in the Panathenaic Stadium, which was originally built in 330 BC and refurbished for the 1896 Olympics.
  • Women were not allowed to compete in the first modern Olympics.
  • The marathon was inspired by the ancient Greek soldier Pheidippides, who ran from the battlefield of Marathon to Athens to announce the Greek victory over the Persians.
